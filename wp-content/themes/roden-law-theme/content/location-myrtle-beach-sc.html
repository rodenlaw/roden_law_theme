Common Causes of Personal Injury in Myrtle Beach

Traffic Accidents on US-17, US-501, and SC-544

US-17 (Kings Highway/Ocean Highway) is the primary artery of the Grand Strand, and US-501 connects Myrtle Beach to Conway and I-95. Both highways experience severe congestion during peak tourist season (May through September) and are among the most dangerous roads in Horry County. T-bone collisions at commercial intersections, rear-end crashes in tourist traffic, and DUI accidents during evening hours account for a significant share of emergency room visits. Our attorneys investigate every crash under S.C. Code § 15-1-300 and pursue claims against negligent, distracted, and impaired drivers.

Water Sports and Beach Activity Injuries

Jet ski rentals, parasailing, banana boat rides, and surfing are staples of the Grand Strand vacation experience — but they come with serious risk. Jet ski collisions, parasailing harness failures, and drowning incidents occur every summer season. Rental operators and tour companies have a duty to maintain equipment, train staff, and warn customers of known dangers. When they fail in that duty, victims may pursue negligence claims and, in cases of extreme recklessness, punitive damages under South Carolina law. Our firm also handles cases arising from rip current drownings and injuries at beachfront pools where adequate lifeguard supervision was lacking.

Hotel, Resort, and Entertainment Venue Injuries

The Grand Strand is lined with hotels, resorts, amusement parks, and entertainment complexes. Slip-and-fall injuries on wet pool decks, balcony falls from improperly maintained railings, elevator malfunctions, food poisoning, and amusement ride injuries give rise to premises liability and product liability claims. Property owners in South Carolina owe a duty of reasonable care to invitees — guests paying to stay, dine, or play on their premises. Our attorneys hold hotel chains, resort operators, and amusement venues accountable when negligent maintenance or insufficient safety measures cause injury.

South Carolina Personal Injury Law — What You Need to Know

Statute of Limitations

South Carolina gives you three years from the date of injury to file a personal injury lawsuit under S.C. Code § 15-3-530. For tourists injured while visiting the Grand Strand, it is important to understand that this deadline applies even if you return to your home state — the lawsuit must be filed in South Carolina within three years. Delay can result in lost evidence and faded witness memories, so we encourage prompt consultation.

Comparative Fault Rule

South Carolina uses a modified comparative fault standard. You can recover compensation as long as your percentage of fault is less than 51%. If your fault reaches 51% or more, you recover nothing. Awards are reduced by your share of responsibility. In Grand Strand cases — particularly water sports and beach activity claims — defendants frequently argue that the victim assumed the risk or was contributorily negligent. Our attorneys anticipate these defenses and build evidence showing the operator's or property owner's primary negligence.

What to Do After an Injury in Myrtle Beach

  1. Seek medical attention. Tidelands Health in Murrells Inlet, Grand Strand Medical Center in Myrtle Beach, and Conway Medical Center provide emergency care in the Grand Strand area. If you are a visitor, do not wait until you return home — get treated immediately and preserve the medical record.
  2. File a report. Report traffic accidents to the Myrtle Beach Police Department, Horry County Police, or the South Carolina Highway Patrol. For water sports incidents, contact the South Carolina Department of Natural Resources (SCDNR). Report hotel/resort injuries to management and request a written incident report.
  3. Document the evidence. Photograph the accident scene, your injuries, equipment involved, and any unsafe conditions. Retain rental agreements, admission tickets, and receipts. Get witness names and contact information.
  4. Do not sign waivers or recorded statements from insurance companies or business operators without consulting an attorney first.

Types of Compensation Available Under South Carolina Law

Grand Strand injury victims can recover economic damages including all medical expenses (emergency, surgical, rehabilitative), lost wages, diminished future earning capacity, and travel costs incurred for treatment. Non-economic damages compensate for pain and suffering, mental anguish, scarring and disfigurement, and loss of enjoyment of life — particularly relevant for visitors whose vacations were destroyed by preventable injuries. In cases involving willful, wanton, or reckless conduct, punitive damages may be awarded under S.C. Code § 15-32-520, generally capped at the greater of three times compensatory damages or $500,000.
